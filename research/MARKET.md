## Overview

| Metric | Value |
|--------|-------|
| Total market volume | CHF 41+ billion/year |
| Federal share | ~20% (CHF 8–9B) |
| Cantonal/municipal share | ~80% (CHF 32–34B) |

**Key insight**: 99% of Swiss businesses are SMEs, yet **90%+ of ICT SMEs** report unfavorable effort-to-success ratio. Over 80% see no improvement since the 2021 BöB/IVöB revision.

## 1. Construction & Infrastructure

**Volume**: CHF 17–20 billion (~45% of market)

### Characteristics
- Largest vertical, driven by infrastructure renewal (CHF 200B planned through 2030)
- Dominated by SBB (CHF 6B+ annual procurement), ASTRA (CHF 2.5B in 2022), cantonal building offices
- Highly standardized: NPK catalog (~1 million positions, 200+ chapters), mandatory IfA18 standard, CRBx exchange format
- Long procurement cycles, large contract values
- BIM adoption at only 18% – significant digitalization runway
- ASTRA awards 81% of contracts via direct award (freihändig)

### Major Procurers
| Entity | Annual Volume | Notes |
|--------|--------------|-------|
| SBB | CHF 6B+ | 360 procurement staff, SAP Ariba |
| ASTRA | CHF 1.9–2.5B | 3,082 contracts (2022) |
| City of Zurich Hochbauamt | ~270 procedures/year | BKP cost classification |

### Existing Solutions
| Platform | Focus | Model | Key Differentiator |
|----------|-------|-------|-------------------|
| **Olmero** | Construction | Freemium | Market leader, 20+ yrs, CHF 5B volume, 2,500+ architects, acquired by Byggfakta (2020, ~€15M) |
| **Devisio** | Construction | Free for bidders | Challenger since 2021, 1,800 tenders, 610 projects, NPK "Offer-Edi" editor |
| **Baublatt Infomanager** | Tender information | Subscription | Construction news and tender alerts |

### Ecosystem Requirements
- CRB certification for NPK integration (40+ certified software solutions)
- SIA 451/CRBx price editors
- Building permit database access
- 7,000+ NPK customers create network effects

### Opportunity
Low-Medium – well-served by Olmero but Devisio's rapid growth (Implenia, Marti, HRS as clients) shows market receptivity to challengers. BIM-to-procurement workflow integration represents untapped opportunity.

## 2. IT Services & Software

**Volume**: CHF 10–12 billion (~25% of market)
**Federal ICT**: CHF 1.3–2.4B annually

### Characteristics
- Fastest growing segment
- BIT (Federal Office of IT) signed CHF 270M in contracts (2023)
- **50% awarded directly** (vs. 10% in other sectors)
- Large framework agreements dominate (CHF 30–50M+ per lot)
- Top suppliers: Swisscom (CHF 129M), SAP (CHF 45M), Atos (CHF 44M)
- Complex requirements, shorter cycles than construction
- Canton Bern ZBS ICT: framework agreements with 3-8 partners per lot

### Existing Solutions
| Platform | Focus | Model | Key Differentiator |
|----------|-------|-------|-------------------|
| **IntelliProcure** | Analytics/research | CHF 200–800/year | 2.1M+ documents, 5.2TB archive, BFH-operated, NLP/AI analysis |
| **it-beschaffung.ch** | IT tender archive | Free | 100,000+ IT procurement records, Noematica GmbH |
| **Tender Service** | All sectors | Subscription | AI "TenderChat", 25+ countries, instant eligibility checks |
| **DTAD** | Pan-European | Subscription | 5,500+ customers, German only |

### Opportunity
Medium – high direct-award rate limits open tender volume, but growing digital transformation demand. **90%+ SME dissatisfaction** with participation economics creates tool opportunity.

## 3. Healthcare & Medical

**Volume**: CHF 3–4 billion (~8% of market)

### Characteristics
- Fragmented across 26 cantonal health systems
- Hospitals, care facilities, cantonal health departments
- Mix of supplies (equipment, pharma) and services
- Varying procurement sophistication by canton
- Emerging consolidation through joint purchasing

### Existing Solutions
| Platform | Focus | Model | Key Differentiator |
|----------|-------|-------|-------------------|
| **CAIB** | Hospital joint purchasing | Consortium | Geneva HUG + Lausanne CHUV |
| **Sana Suisse Med AG** | Hospital network | Group purchasing | 30 Swiss hospitals, German Sana network (€3B+ volume) |
| **hystrix medical** | Healthcare e-commerce | SaaS | ERP integration, claims 5-10% better discounts, 50-70% time savings |

### Opportunity
High – fragmentation creates discovery problems; hystrix model shows viability of vertical-specific solutions. Hospital procurement events via procure.ch indicate professionalization trend.

## 4. Professional Services

**Volume**: CHF 2–3 billion (~6% of market)

### Characteristics
- Consulting, legal, financial advisory, engineering
- Often below publication thresholds
- Reference requirements disadvantage newcomers
- Growing demand from digital transformation projects
- "Consulting for free" requirements cited as major SME barrier

### Existing Solutions
| Platform | Focus | Model |
|----------|-------|-------|
| **DTAD** | Pan-European | Subscription |
| **Tender Service** | All sectors | Subscription |
| **GlobalTenders** | International | Subscription |

### Opportunity
Medium-High – SMEs struggle with reference requirements; consortium tools could unlock participation. No dedicated Swiss platform.

## 5. Transportation & Logistics

**Volume**: CHF 2–3 billion (~6% of market)
**Public transport subsidies**: CHF 2.2B annually

### Characteristics
- Public transport operators (PostAuto, BLS, regional carriers)
- Significant standalone procurement: VBZ (Zurich) tendered CHF 140M for 100 e-buses
- Freight and logistics for federal agencies
- Vehicle procurement, maintenance contracts
- Seasonal patterns (fleet renewals)
- No coordinated platform across operators

### Existing Solutions
Covered by generic platforms, no specialist.

### Opportunity
Medium – niche but consistent volume. Fragmented operator landscape creates aggregation opportunity.

## 6. Environment & Sustainability

**Volume**: CHF 1–2 billion (~4%, growing)

### Characteristics
- New priority since 2021 legal reform
- **Minimum 10% weighting** for sustainability criteria mandated
- Current adoption only ~7.2% (Vergabemonitor data)
- Waste management, remediation, renewable energy
- Limited historical data for benchmarking
- Tools focus on compliance verification, not procurement

### Existing Solutions
| Platform | Focus | Model |
|----------|-------|-------|
| **WöB** | Knowledge platform | Free | Wissensplattform nachhaltige öffentliche Beschaffung |
| **EcoVadis** | Supplier ratings | Subscription | Sustainability verification |
| **amfori BSCI** | Social compliance | Membership | Supply chain audits |
| **Logib** | Wage equality | Free | Federal compliance tool |

### Opportunity
High – regulatory tailwind (10% mandate vs 7.2% current), no integrated procurement platform, growing budget allocation.

## 7. Education & Research

### Characteristics
- Universities, ETH domain, cantonal schools

- Often specialized/technical requirements

### Existing Solutions

|----------|-------|-------|-------------------|
| **KoBe ETH+** | ETH domain consortium | Internal | Since 2003: ETH Zürich, EPFL, EMPA, PSI, others |

### Opportunity
Medium – specialized requirements benefit from intelligent matching. Consortium model could extend to cantonal universities.

## SME Pain Points (Validated by Swico 2024 Survey)

1. **Administrative burden** – Effort disproportionate to contract value (90%+ cite as "killer criterion")
2. **Reference requirements** – Catch-22 for newcomers
3. **Price weighting** – Often 80% price / 20% quality despite law revision intent
4. **"Consulting for free"** – Unpaid proposal effort
5. **Language barriers** – DE/FR/IT fragmentation, no AI translation
6. **Discovery fatigue** – Must monitor multiple language searches separately
7. **Direct awards** – 50% of IT contracts, 81% of ASTRA contracts never published
8. **No improvement perceived** – 80%+ see no change since 2021 reform

## Regulatory Environment

### 2021 Reform (BöB/IVöB Revision)
- Mandatory sustainability weighting (10% minimum)
- Lot division encouraged for SME access
- Electronic procurement mandated
- Quality criteria emphasis (vs pure price)

### Current Reality
- Sustainability criteria adoption: ~7.2% (below mandate)
- Price weighting still dominates in practice
- SIMAP 2024 platform refresh added e-submission
- 26 cantonal variations in implementation

### Key Thresholds (WTO/GPA)
- Supplies/services: CHF 230,000+
- Construction: CHF 8.7M+
- Above threshold = mandatory SIMAP publication

## Market Entry Recommendation

### Target Verticals (Priority Order)

1. **Healthcare** – Fragmented, hystrix proves model, Sana consolidation trend
2. **Environment** – Regulatory tailwind (10% mandate vs 7.2% actual), no incumbent
3. **Professional Services** – SME-heavy, consortium potential, no Swiss platform
4. **IT Services** – Growing volume, 90%+ dissatisfied SMEs, it-beschaffung.ch only aggregates

### Secondary Opportunities

5. **Construction** – Devisio proves challenger viability, BIM integration gap
6. **Transportation** – Fragmented operators, no aggregation platform

### Avoid Initially

- **Enterprise procurement** – SAP Ariba/JAGGAER entrenched
- **Pure aggregation** – Commoditized, low margins

### Positioning

> "Intelligent tender matching for Swiss SMEs – cross-sector, cross-lingual, mobile-first"

### Differentiation

- SME-appropriate filters (contract size, reference requirements, price weighting %)
- All three Swiss languages with AI translation (DE/FR/IT)
- Freemium model undercutting CHF 200–800/yr incumbents
- Mobile-first design (vs SIMAP "Windows 98" app)
- Consortium matching with template contracts
- ERP integration (Bexio, Abacus)
- Win probability scoring based on historical patterns

### Strategic Entry Vectors

1. **SME-first mobile platform** – Address 90%+ dissatisfaction
2. **Multilingual AI matching** – Cross-language tender discovery
3. **Consortium formation SaaS** – Template contracts, capability matching
4. **BIM-to-procurement workflow** – Bridge 18% digitalization gap
5. **Vertical-specific solutions** – Healthcare or IT services
